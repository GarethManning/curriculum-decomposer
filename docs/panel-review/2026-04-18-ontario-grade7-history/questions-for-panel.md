# Questions for the expert panel

Each panelist is asked to review the Ontario Grade 7 History harness output and address the following. Please reason in the open, score the output out of 100, and name what would push it to 90+.

1. Does the KUD produced by the harness represent the Grade 7 History curriculum a reviewer would recognise? Is it at the right grade? The right domain? The right grain for teacher planning?

2. Are the Learning Targets at consistent teacher-assessment grain? Would a Grade 7 History teacher use these as-is, edit them, or reject them?

3. Does the output capture the disciplinary thinking that Ontario's Grade 7 History curriculum requires — historical inquiry, perspective-taking, evidence analysis, First Nations/Metis/Inuit ways of knowing, continuity-and-change reasoning — or does it reduce history to a list of facts?

4. Would an AI tutoring system consuming this output produce useful adaptive tutoring for a Grade 7 History student, or would it serve wrong content with confidence?

5. Specific invention-or-omission: name anything in the output that shouldn't be there (invented content not in the source) or that's missing (source content not represented in the output).

6. What would push this output to 90+ on a scale where 100 is "a practising Ontario Grade 7 History teacher would adopt this without meaningful editing"? What's the gap?
