## Competency 1 — Emotional Intelligence
*The ability to understand, express, and manage one's emotions while empathetically responding to others.*

### LT 1.1 — Self-Awareness & Regulation
**Definition:** I can notice and manage my emotions, thoughts, and behaviours.
**Type:** T3 — Dispositional | **Assessment:** Multi-informant observation; Reflection 360; not summatively graded.

| Band | Learning Group | Observation Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can notice what my body is telling me, name a feeling, and use one calming strategy to return to learning. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can identify a common personal trigger and choose a practised strategy to manage my response. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can apply a chosen strategy in a challenging situation and reflect on how well it worked. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can apply my strategies under stress across different settings, explain what worked and what didn't, and state one change for next time. |

### LT 1.2 — Social Awareness & Empathy
**Definition:** I can understand others' feelings and respond with care.
**Type:** T3 — Dispositional | **Assessment:** Multi-informant observation; Reflection 360; not summatively graded.

| Band | Learning Group | Observation Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can name a feeling another person might be having and offer a kind word or a specific helpful action. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can describe a situation from another person's perspective and ask if they would like help before acting. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can notice what others say and do in a group, check in when I'm unsure, and adjust my actions to match their stated needs or boundaries. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can repair a relationship after conflict by listening, owning my part, and agreeing on a next step that works for both people. |

## Competency 2 — Attention & Reflective Practices
*The ability to develop present attention and reflection for clarity and wise action.*
*Note: Previously titled "Mindfulness & Reflective Practices." Renamed following the MYRIAD RCT (Kuyken et al., 2022). The science of attention and a repertoire of strategies is well-supported; claiming to develop a mindfulness practice through weekly curriculum sessions is not.*

### LT 2.1 — Focused Attention & Strategy
**Definition:** I can direct and sustain my attention purposefully and return it when it wanders.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; reflective tasks applied to real situations.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can use my senses to attend to one task for a short time and describe how I brought my attention back when it wandered. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can use a practised attention strategy during a task and describe what made it easier or harder to focus. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can evaluate how an attention strategy affected my focus during a specific task and explain why it did or didn't help. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can sustain attention during a challenging task, identify what is pulling it away, and use a deliberate strategy to return. |

### LT 2.2 — Reflective Decision-Making
**Definition:** I can reflect on my experiences and make choices that fit my values.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; novel scenario tasks; written analysis.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can retell a short experience and state one thing I learned from it. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can stop and state at least one helpful and one harmful possible outcome before making a choice. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can compare multiple options, state my choice, and give a reason based on my values. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can explain the trade-offs in a decision and justify my choice by considering how it affects different people. |

## Competency 3 — Physical Wellbeing & Self-Care
*The ability to understand and apply health-enhancing practices and routines that build resilience.*

### LT 3.1 — Health Literacy & Habits
**Definition:** I can understand and use healthy routines.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; habit design and evaluation tasks; Strive data used as evidence within reasoning tasks.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can name my body's basic needs and practise simple personal hygiene routines with reminders. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can explain how sleep, food, and movement affect how I feel, and set a goal for one healthy habit. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can track one habit for a week, identify a barrier, and explain why I want to keep or change it. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can compare two sources of health information, create a balanced weekly habits plan, and adjust it based on logged results. |

### LT 3.2 — Self-Care & Resilience
**Definition:** I can maintain my wellbeing and seek and give support.
**Type:** T3 — Dispositional | **Assessment:** Multi-informant observation; Strive longitudinal data as process evidence; Reflection 360; not summatively graded.

| Band | Learning Group | Observation Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can name a trusted adult I can ask for help and practise a simple self-care routine. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can identify my body's early warning signs and choose a helpful self-care response. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can make a self-care plan that works at home and school and list trusted people or services for support. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can maintain my key self-care routines during stressful times and describe one way I support a peer to do the same. |

## Competency 4 — Consent, Safety & Healthy Relationships
*The ability to understand bodies, boundaries, consent, puberty, and safe, respectful choices; accessing trustworthy health information.*
*Note: Previously titled "Sexual Health & Respectful Relationships." Renamed to clarify the competency structure. The two LTs remain unchanged. For this competency, scenario-based assessment is not a workaround — it is the appropriate and most defensible approach throughout.*

### LT 4.1 — Bodies, Boundaries & Consent
**Definition:** I can respect bodies and boundaries, seek and give consent, and get help when safety is unclear.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; novel scenario tasks; structured roleplay. Specialist delivery recommended from Band C.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can name private body parts correctly and say who my trusted adults are that help keep me safe. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can set and respect boundaries, ask for clear consent, and seek help if I feel pressured. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can apply consent skills in non-sexual and online contexts and state my boundaries clearly to peers. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can recognise power or peer-pressure risks, state my refusal and exit plan, and describe how I would support a peer to access help. |

### LT 4.2 — Puberty, Health & Safe Choices
**Definition:** I can understand puberty and sexual health basics and make safe, respectful choices.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; written analysis; scenario tasks. Specialist delivery required at Bands C and D.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | N/A — not applicable at this developmental stage. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can explain the basic changes of puberty, use personal hygiene routines, and find reliable health information from trusted sources. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can manage my personal hygiene related to puberty, identify trusted sources of health information, and explain the basics of human reproduction. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can assess risks related to STIs, pregnancy, and substance use, explain protection methods, and identify steps to access confidential health advice. |

## Competency 5 — Community, Purpose & Belonging
*The ability to foster healthy relationships and inclusive, purpose-driven communities.*
*Note: Previously titled "Community & Relationship Building." Renamed to foreground purpose and belonging as outcomes, not just process.*

### LT 5.1 — Interpersonal Skills & Communication
**Definition:** I can build healthy relationships with clear, respectful communication.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; structured roleplay; novel conflict scenario tasks.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can take turns in conversation, use kind words, and use an I-statement. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can use I-statements to state my needs, ask about boundaries, and propose two options for a fair solution. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can listen in a disagreement without interrupting, choose words that stay respectful, and use a simple repair routine. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can state my boundaries clearly, paraphrase before I respond, and choose words that fit the situation including in conflict. |

### LT 5.2 — Community Engagement & Purpose
**Definition:** I can contribute to community wellbeing with purpose.
**Type:** T3 — Dispositional | **Assessment:** Multi-informant observation; D2R project evidence where coordination exists; LD capstone as primary Band D evidence context; Reflection 360; not summatively graded.

| Band | Learning Group | Observation Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can do a classroom job and explain how it helps our class. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can identify when someone is left out and invite them to join an activity. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can identify a need in our school, propose a simple project, and complete it with a team. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can work with a diverse group to address a need, show the impact of our project, and explain why the work matters to me. |

## Competency 6 — Wellbeing Science & Literacy *(new)*
*The ability to understand how the brain and body produce the experiences of stress, emotion, habit, and attention — and to evaluate health information using this knowledge.*
*This competency provides the T1 knowledge prerequisites that Emotional Intelligence (LT 1.1) and Physical Wellbeing & Self-Care (LT 3.1, 3.2) are knowledge-contingent upon. It is the formal home for science content already being taught but not previously assessed.*

### LT 6.1 — Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science
**Definition:** I can explain how the brain, body, and nervous system produce the experiences of stress, emotion, habit, and attention.
**Type:** T1 — Hierarchical | **Assessment:** Rubric with clear criteria; structured explanation tasks; vocabulary checks. *No prerequisite — this LT is itself foundational.*

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can name body signals that tell me something about how I feel, and describe what sleep and food do for my body and brain. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can explain what happens in my body during a fight, flight, or freeze response, and describe the three parts of the habit loop. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can explain how the amygdala and prefrontal cortex interact during a stressful moment, explain what neuroplasticity means, and describe how the habit loop explains behaviour change at the level of mechanism. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can use accurate neuroscience vocabulary to explain how stress, emotion, attention, and habit interact as a system, and describe the role of the HPA axis in the stress response. |

### LT 6.2 — Health Information Literacy
**Definition:** I can evaluate health information by examining the quality of its evidence and the reliability of its source.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; scenario tasks using real health claims; written analysis. *Prerequisite: LT 6.1 — students need foundational neuroscience knowledge to evaluate claims meaningfully.*

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can identify whether a health claim comes from a trusted adult or source and explain my reasoning. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can compare two health information sources and describe which one I trust more and why. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can analyse a health claim by examining the type of evidence it uses and identifying whether the source has a reason to be biased. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can evaluate a contested health claim by comparing the quality of evidence on each side, identifying the strongest and weakest arguments, and justifying my conclusion. |

## Competency 7 — Metacognitive Self-Direction *(new)*
*The ability to notice, analyse, and intentionally adjust one's own thinking and learning patterns.*
*Previously embedded within Competency 2. Now a standalone competency because its developmental arc and assessment requirements are distinct. Metacognitive interventions have among the highest effect sizes in education research (Hattie, 2009). This competency is what makes all other wellbeing learning transferable: a student who cannot notice their own patterns cannot apply what they have learned about emotion, habit, or decision-making to their actual life.*

### LT 7.1 — Pattern Analysis & Adjustment
**Definition:** I can analyse a pattern in my own thinking or behaviour, explain what drives it, and describe a specific adjustment I have made or will make.
**Type:** T2 — Horizontal | **Assessment:** Reasoning-quality rubric; structured reflective tasks; portfolio entries applied to real situations. Transfer is the test.

| Band | Learning Group | Band Statement |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | I can describe one thing I noticed about how I reacted to a situation and say what I might do differently next time. |
| B | Earth Dragons | I can identify a pattern in how I tend to respond in a type of situation and give a reason for why that might happen. |
| C | Fire Dragons | I can analyse a pattern across more than one situation, explain what drives it, and describe a specific adjustment I have made or will make. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | I can evaluate a pattern in my thinking or responses across different contexts, justify what sustains it, and describe the effect of a specific adjustment I have made. |

### LT 7.2 — Self-Direction in Practice
**Definition:** The student enacts metacognitive self-direction as a sustained orientation — noticing patterns and adjusting their approach habitually across contexts. *Assessed through multi-informant observation; not summatively graded.*
**Type:** T3 — Dispositional | **Assessment:** Multi-informant observation; student self-reflection; Reflection 360 developmental conversation. *Prerequisite: LT 7.1 — students need the T2 analytical capability before this disposition can be meaningfully observed.*

| Band | Learning Group | Observation Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| A | Water, Air Dragons | Stops and tries a different approach when the first attempt does not work, without being told to. After a setback, names what happened rather than dismissing it. Accepts a suggestion to try differently without extended resistance. |
| B | Earth Dragons | Refers back to a strategy they have previously found helpful, without being prompted. After a task, volunteers one specific observation about how they worked. When something goes wrong, asks a question about why rather than placing blame. |
| C | Fire Dragons | Identifies a pattern in how they tend to respond across more than one situation and names it spontaneously in conversation. Adjusts their approach mid-task in response to noticing something is not working. In reflective writing, connects a current response to a pattern they have identified previously. |
| D | Metal, Light Dragons | Articulates a specific adjustment they have made to their thinking or behaviour and describes its effect across more than one domain. In novel or challenging situations, applies a self-awareness strategy without an adult prompt. Makes connections between wellbeing learning and behaviour in other contexts, unprompted. |
