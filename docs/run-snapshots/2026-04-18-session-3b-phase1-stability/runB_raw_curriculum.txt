History, Grade 7

Overall Expectations

By the end of Grade 7, students will:

A1. Application: Investigating Historical Developments
analyse significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800, and explain their short- and long-term impacts on different individuals, groups, and/or regions (FOCUS ON: Cause and Consequence)

A2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation
use the historical inquiry process to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800, and to develop and support historically defensible arguments about their causes, consequences, and significance (FOCUS ON: Historical Significance)

A3. Understanding Historical Context: New France and British North America, 1713–1800
demonstrate an understanding of significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800, including the perspectives of different individuals and groups (FOCUS ON: Historical Perspective)

B1. Application: Investigating Historical Developments
analyse significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850, and explain their short- and long-term impacts on different individuals, groups, and/or regions (FOCUS ON: Cause and Consequence)

B2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation
use the historical inquiry process to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850, and to develop and support historically defensible arguments about their causes, consequences, and significance (FOCUS ON: Historical Significance)

B3. Understanding Historical Context: Canada, 1800–1850: Conflict and Challenges
demonstrate an understanding of significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850, including the perspectives of different individuals and groups (FOCUS ON: Historical Perspective)

Specific Expectations

A. New France and British North America, 1713–1800

A1. Application: Investigating Historical Developments

A1.1 analyse the short- and long-term impacts of contact between First Nations, Métis, Inuit, and European explorers and settlers on the different groups involved (e.g., the impact of the fur trade on First Nations economies and societies; the impact of disease on Indigenous populations; the impact of the Indian Act on First Nations communities; the impact of colonization on Métis and Inuit communities; the impact of European settlement on Indigenous land use and governance systems)

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What were some of the major short- and long-term consequences for the Haudenosaunee and Anishinaabe people of contact with European explorers and settlers?
• How did the fur trade change the way First Nations peoples lived?
• What impact did European diseases have on Indigenous populations?
• How did colonization affect Métis identity and communities?

A1.2 analyse the causes and consequences of key events and developments in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800 (e.g., the Treaty of Utrecht; the Seven Years' War; the Conquest of New France; the Royal Proclamation of 1763; Pontiac's Resistance; the American Revolution; the arrival of the Loyalists; the Constitutional Act of 1791)

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What factors led to the Seven Years' War? What were the consequences of this war for different groups?
• How did the Royal Proclamation of 1763 affect First Nations peoples?
• Why did Pontiac lead a resistance? What was the outcome?
• How did the arrival of the Loyalists change British North America?

A1.3 explain the significance of the fur trade in the development of New France and British North America, including its impact on First Nations, Métis, and European communities

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• Why was the fur trade so important to the economy of New France?
• How did the fur trade create new relationships between First Nations and Europeans?
• What role did Métis people play in the fur trade?

A2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation

A2.1 formulate questions to guide investigations into significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800 (e.g., questions about the causes and consequences of key events, the perspectives of different groups, the significance of developments)

A2.2 gather and organize evidence from a variety of primary and secondary sources, including community sources, to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800

A2.3 analyse and interpret evidence from primary and secondary sources to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800, using concepts of historical thinking

A2.4 evaluate evidence and draw conclusions about significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800

A2.5 communicate the results of historical investigations using appropriate historical terminology and formats

A3. Understanding Historical Context: New France and British North America, 1713–1800

A3.1 demonstrate an understanding of the significance of Pontiac's Resistance, including the factors that led to it and its impact on First Nations and British policy

A3.2 demonstrate an understanding of the significance of key treaties (e.g., Treaty of Utrecht, Treaty of Niagara, 1760 Treaty of Peace and Friendship) and their impacts on different groups

A3.3 demonstrate an understanding of the experiences and perspectives of First Nations, Métis, Inuit, and European communities in New France and British North America from 1713 to 1800

A3.4 demonstrate an understanding of the role of the fur trade in shaping the development of New France and British North America

A3.5 demonstrate an understanding of the causes and consequences of the Seven Years' War and the Conquest of New France

A3.6 demonstrate an understanding of the significance of the Royal Proclamation of 1763 for First Nations peoples

A3.7 demonstrate an understanding of the causes and consequences of the American Revolution and the arrival of the Loyalists in British North America

B. Canada, 1800–1850: Conflict and Challenges

B1. Application: Investigating Historical Developments

B1.1 analyse the short- and long-term impacts of colonization, including forced displacement and assimilation policies, on First Nations, Métis, and Inuit individuals and communities (e.g., the impact of the Indian Act; the impact of residential schools; the impact of the Gradual Enfranchisement Act; the impact of the numbered treaties)

B1.2 analyse the causes and consequences of key events and developments in Canada from 1800 to 1850 (e.g., the War of 1812; the Rebellions of 1837–38; immigration and settlement; the development of the fur trade; the building of canals; the development of agriculture)

B1.3 explain the significance of the development of transportation and communication systems in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation

B2.1 formulate questions to guide investigations into significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B2.2 gather and organize evidence from a variety of primary and secondary sources, including community sources, to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B2.3 analyse and interpret evidence from primary and secondary sources to investigate significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850, using concepts of historical thinking

B2.4 evaluate evidence and draw conclusions about significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B2.5 communicate the results of historical investigations using appropriate historical terminology and formats

B3. Understanding Historical Context: Canada, 1800–1850: Conflict and Challenges

B3.1 demonstrate an understanding of the causes and consequences of the Rebellions of 1837–38, including the perspectives of different groups involved

B3.2 demonstrate an understanding of the significance of the War of 1812 for different groups in Canada

B3.3 demonstrate an understanding of the experiences and perspectives of First Nations, Métis, Inuit, and settler communities in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B3.4 demonstrate an understanding of immigration and settlement patterns in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B3.5 demonstrate an understanding of the development of transportation and communication systems in Canada from 1800 to 1850

B3.6 demonstrate an understanding of the role of the fur trade in the economy of Canada from 1800 to 1850
