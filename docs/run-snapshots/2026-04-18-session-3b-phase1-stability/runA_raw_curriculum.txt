History, Grade 7

Overall Expectations

By the end of Grade 7, students will:

A1. Application: Analyzing Historical Developments
explain the significance of some key developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800, and analyse the impact of these developments, events, and/or issues on different groups, including First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples (FOCUS ON: Historical Significance)

A2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation
use the historical inquiry process to investigate some significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800, and to develop and support historically defensible arguments about their causes, consequences, and significance (FOCUS ON: Cause and Consequence)

A3. Understanding Historical Context: New France and British North America, 1713–1800
demonstrate an understanding of some key developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800, including the experiences of and challenges facing different groups, particularly First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples

B1. Application: Analyzing Historical Developments
explain the significance of some key developments, events, and/or issues in Canada between 1800 and 1850, and analyse the impact of these developments, events, and/or issues on different groups, including First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples (FOCUS ON: Historical Significance)

B2. Inquiry: Historical Investigation
use the historical inquiry process to investigate some significant developments, events, and/or issues in Canada between 1800 and 1850, and to develop and support historically defensible arguments about their causes, consequences, and significance (FOCUS ON: Cause and Consequence)

B3. Understanding Historical Context: Canada, 1800–1850: Conflict and Challenges
demonstrate an understanding of some key developments, events, and/or issues in Canada between 1800 and 1850, including the experiences of and challenges facing different groups, particularly First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples

Specific Expectations

A1.1 explain the significance of some key developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800 (e.g., the Treaty of Utrecht; the fur trade; the Seven Years' War; Pontiac's Resistance; the Royal Proclamation of 1763; the Quebec Act; the American Revolution; the arrival of Loyalists; the Constitutional Act of 1791), and analyse how these developments, events, and/or issues affected different groups, including First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples, in different ways

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What factors led to Pontiac's Resistance? How successful was this resistance? Why is it significant for First Nations?
• How did the Royal Proclamation of 1763 affect First Nations peoples? How did it affect European settlers?
• What were some of the consequences of the Seven Years' War for First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples?
• How did the arrival of Loyalists affect the territories of First Nations and Métis peoples?

A1.2 analyse the impact of the fur trade on First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples, European traders, and the environment during this period

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• How did the fur trade change the way First Nations peoples lived?
• What role did Métis peoples play in the fur trade?
• How did the fur trade affect the natural environment?
• How did the fur trade create new relationships between First Nations, Métis, and European peoples?

A1.3 describe some of the central values and world views of First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples during this period, and analyse how these values and world views were reflected in their interactions with the environment and with European peoples

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What were some central values and world views of Inuit in the eighteenth century? What are some ways in which these values and world views are reflected in present-day Inuit communities?
• How did First Nations peoples' relationship with the land differ from that of European settlers?
• What role did spirituality play in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures?

A1.4 analyse the role of women in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit societies during this period, and compare their roles with those of women in European societies of the same era

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What roles did women play in First Nations societies?
• How did the fur trade affect the roles and status of Métis women?
• How did the roles of women in First Nations societies differ from those of women in European societies?

A2.1 formulate questions to guide investigations into some significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800 (e.g., formulate questions about the causes and consequences of the Seven Years' War, Pontiac's Resistance, or the arrival of Loyalists; formulate questions about how different groups experienced these events)

A2.2 gather and organize evidence and information from a variety of primary and secondary sources, including community sources, to investigate some significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800 (e.g., use letters, diaries, maps, government documents, oral histories, and websites to gather information about the fur trade, treaties, or the experiences of different groups during this period)

A2.3 analyse maps and construct maps, as part of their investigations, to help them understand the geographic context of some significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800 (e.g., construct a map showing the extent of New France; analyse maps showing the territories of First Nations peoples; construct a map showing the routes of the fur trade)

A2.4 interpret and analyse evidence and information relevant to their investigations, using a variety of tools and strategies (e.g., use a timeline to sequence events; use a graphic organizer to compare the perspectives of different groups on an event; use a chart to organize information about the causes and consequences of an event)

A2.5 evaluate evidence and information, and draw conclusions about some significant developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800, and about their causes, consequences, and significance

A2.6 communicate the results of their historical inquiries, using appropriate historical terminology and evidence to support their conclusions (e.g., write an essay explaining the significance of an event; create a presentation comparing the perspectives of different groups on an issue; write a historical account from the perspective of a person living during this period)

A3.1 describe some of the key developments, events, and/or issues in New France and British North America between 1713 and 1800, including:
• the political, economic, and social structures of New France
• the fur trade and its impact on First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples
• the Seven Years' War and its consequences
• Pontiac's Resistance and its significance
• the Royal Proclamation of 1763 and its impact on First Nations peoples
• the Quebec Act and its significance
• the American Revolution and the arrival of Loyalists in British North America
• the Constitutional Act of 1791 and the creation of Upper and Lower Canada

A3.2 describe the experiences of and challenges facing First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples during this period, including:
• the impact of European contact and colonization
• the fur trade and its effects on their ways of life
• the loss of territories and resources
• the disruption of traditional practices and beliefs
• the role of treaties in their relationships with European powers and later with the British and Canadian governments

A3.3 describe the experiences of and challenges facing European settlers and Loyalists during this period, including:
• the reasons for European settlement in New France and British North America
• the establishment of communities and economic activities
• the impact of the American Revolution on Loyalists
• the settlement of Loyalists in British North America

A3.4 describe the role of key individuals and groups in shaping developments, events, and/or issues during this period (e.g., fur traders, First Nations leaders, Métis leaders, Loyalist leaders, government officials)

B1.1 explain the significance of some key developments, events, and/or issues in Canada between 1800 and 1850 (e.g., the War of 1812; the fur trade decline; immigration and settlement; the development of the timber trade; the building of canals; the Rebellions of 1837–38; the Act of Union of 1840), and analyse how these developments, events, and/or issues affected different groups, including First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples, in different ways

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• What were some of the key social, economic, and political issues that led to the Rebellions of 1837–38?
• How did immigration and settlement affect First Nations and Métis peoples?
• What role did the timber trade play in the development of Canada?
• How did the building of canals affect communities and the environment?

B1.2 analyse the impact of immigration and settlement on First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples, and on the environment, during this period

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• How did European settlement affect the territories and ways of life of First Nations peoples?
• What challenges did First Nations peoples face as European settlement expanded?
• How did the fur trade decline affect Métis peoples?

B1.3 describe some of the central values and world views of First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples during this period, and analyse how colonialism and European settlement affected these values and world views

Teacher supports

Sample Questions
• How did colonialism affect First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures and identities?
• What strategies did First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples use to resist colonialism and maintain their cultures?
