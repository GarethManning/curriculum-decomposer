# Crosswalk — REAL Wellbeing × UK Statutory RSHE × Welsh CfW × CASEL SEL × Circle Solutions

**Scope**: REAL Bands A–F (all six bands). **Compared frameworks**: REAL School Budapest Wellbeing Framework (band-tagged v1, 2026-04); UK DfE Statutory RSHE, July 2025 (band-tagged v1, 2026-04); Welsh Curriculum for Wales — Health and Well-being AoLE, Descriptions of Learning per Progression Step (band-tagged v2, 2026-04); CASEL SEL Skills Continuum, January 2023 (band-tagged v2, 2026-04 — CW-2b, Band F restored); Circle Solutions SEL Framework — Cowie & Myers (2016), Assessment Framework observable indicators for Year 2, Year 6, Year 9, Year 12 (band-tagged v2, 2026-04 — CW-2b, pipeline-generated).

**PLC context**: REAL School Budapest wellbeing team reviewing coverage and sequencing of the REAL wellbeing framework across bands A–F against four external frameworks — UK statutory RSHE, Welsh CfW Health & Wellbeing, CASEL SEL Skills Continuum, and the Circle Solutions SEL Assessment Framework — in preparation for PLC review and framework finalisation.

---

**Note on upstream band ambiguity**: UK RSHE content is tagged to either "End of Primary" (→ REAL Bands A–C, all items ambiguous across the full primary phase) or "End of Secondary" (→ REAL Bands C–E, all items ambiguous across the full secondary phase). Welsh CfW Progression Step 1 maps unambiguously to Band A; all other PS mappings carry a two-band span: PS2→[B,C], PS3→[C,D], PS4→[D,E], PS5→[E,F]. CASEL grade bands map cleanly to REAL bands with high confidence (Pre-K→A pre-band; K-1→A; 2-3→B; 4-5→C; 6-8→D; 9-10→E; 11-12→F). Circle Solutions year levels carry an ambiguity flag: Year 2→[A,B] (age 7), Year 6→C (age 10-11), Year 9→[D,E] (age 13-14), Year 12→F (age 16-17). Rows in the tables below preserve all candidate bands; band-gap calculations use midpoints of ambiguous ranges.

**Note on Band E/F REAL content**: The 28 items in REAL Bands E and F were newly authored in session REAL-2a (2026-04-20) and have not yet been through full REAL School teacher review. They appear in the tables below where relevant and are marked *(draft — teacher review pending)*.

**Note on CW-2b updates (v2 changes from v1)**:
- *CASEL Band F restored*: CASEL Grade Band 11-12 (→ REAL Band F) produced 0 KUD items in v1 due to API credit exhaustion causing `call_or_parse_failed` on all three classification runs — not genuine T2/T3 ambiguity. CW-2b re-ran the pipeline after API credit top-up. Band F now has 28 KUD items and 14 LTs. New CASEL Band F convergence rows appear in Section 1.
- *Circle Solutions pipeline-generated*: The v1 crosswalk used manually constructed band-tagged data for Circle Solutions (CW-2 was interrupted before pipeline completion). CW-2b re-ran the full pipeline for all four year-level strands. The v2 source has 124 KUD items and 104 LTs (versus 112/48 in the manually constructed v1). CS quotes in this crosswalk use v2 pipeline LT definitions rather than raw observable indicator text from the source document.

**Note on voice conventions**: REAL content appears in REAL's "I can / Students can" voice. RSHE content appears in the statutory propositional register. Welsh CfW content appears in its "I can / I have" first-person learner voice. CASEL content alternates between "I can" items (K/U knowledge) and dispositional orientation statements (D dispositional). Circle Solutions content appears as pipeline-generated learning target definitions in first-person and third-person performance voice. No statements have been rewritten or paraphrased; all quotations are verbatim or close paraphrase from the band-tagged sources.

## 4. Sequencing Differences

**Emotional regulation and self-awareness.** All five frameworks sequence emotion regulation from simple to complex, but with different grain and pacing. REAL has a 6-band explicit progression from naming one feeling (Band A) to articulating a personal regulation system and supporting others (Band F). Welsh CfW has a parallel 5-step progression, tracking closely with REAL. CASEL has a 6-band progression (Pre-K/K-1 through Grade 11-12) that closely mirrors REAL's sequencing with an equivalent grain size — the two frameworks are the most closely aligned on regulation sequencing of any pair in this crosswalk. At Band F, CASEL completes with complex emotion identification and personal development evaluation ("I can identify complex emotions and explain how personal and external factors influence emotional responses") while REAL completes with an articulated personal regulation system deployed in support of others — REAL's terminal outcome is more system-level and relational. Circle Solutions' 4-year-level structure maps regulation content at Year 2, 6, 9, and 12, with less fine-grained developmental staging between levels. UK RSHE has no fine-grained regulation progression.

**Consent and body autonomy.** REAL introduces body ownership and trusted adults at Band A (LT 4.1), moves to boundary-setting and consent skills at Band B, generalises consent online at Band C, addresses power and peer pressure at Band D, applies legal consent knowledge at Band E, and reaches peer-education capacity at Band F. UK RSHE introduces body rights and boundaries at End of Primary (A–C) and legal/ethical consent at End of Secondary (C–E) — a 2-phase approach. CASEL has no consent or body-autonomy strand (across all six bands). Circle Solutions addresses assertiveness from Year 2 and peer-pressure resistance at Year 12, but has no explicit consent progression. A notable difference: RSHE places grooming awareness firmly in the primary phase (A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of grooming and exploitation risk appears at Band E (LT 4.1) — a 2-band gap.

**Neuroscience education.** REAL begins introducing body-brain connections at Band A and sequences amygdala-PFC interaction at Band C-D, escalating to HPA axis and stress system (Band D), wellbeing intervention evaluation (Band E), and contested claims (Band F). Circle Solutions introduces amygdala function at Year 9 (Band D/E) — later than REAL's Band C-D introduction, and at a simpler grain (recognition without mechanism). CASEL, RSHE, and CfW have no neuroscience strand. REAL's neuroscience progression is the most analytically ambitious element of its framework and is unique in scope and depth across all five frameworks.

**Decision-making.** REAL's 6-band decision-making progression (LT 2.2) moves from single-observation reflection (Band A) to values-anchored choice (Band C) to bias identification and epistemic humility (Band E) to reasoned positions under irreducible uncertainty (Band F). CASEL runs the most closely parallel progression: from consequence recognition (Grade 2-3) to structured decision-making models with impact analysis (Grade 6-8) to evidence-gathering and ethical reasoning (Grade 9-10) to systematic critical thinking in high-stakes situations (Grade 11-12, Band F). The two frameworks track closely across all six bands, with REAL slightly more epistemologically demanding at the terminal bands (acknowledging irreducible uncertainty vs. applying critical thinking processes). Welsh CfW's WM3 is also closely aligned, with CfW being slightly more collective-minded. Circle Solutions addresses decision-making through its conflict-resolution and meaning strands rather than a dedicated decision-making progression. RSHE embeds decision-making in relationships content.

**Repair and restoration.** Circle Solutions introduces intentional repair concepts at Year 2 (Band A/B): "I can distinguish between accidental and deliberate hurts" / "I can participate in processes that address harm and work toward healing relationships." REAL's explicit relationship repair skill appears at Band D (LT 1.2), approximately 3 bands later. This represents the largest sequencing gap in the crosswalk. Circle Solutions' early-years repair language may offer a complementary scaffold for REAL, particularly given that REAL students arrive at the school across bands. CASEL, RSHE, and CfW all sequence repair later than Circle Solutions, but earlier than REAL.

**Help-seeking and mental health support.** REAL introduces the concept of a named trusted adult at Band A (LT 3.2) and builds to accessing confidential health services at Band E. CASEL begins help-seeking at Pre-K (safety resources, identifying community helpers) and escalates to seeking appropriate support for academic and personal challenges through Band E. Both REAL and CASEL sequence help-seeking from Band A, and their parallel progression is closer than with RSHE or CfW. Welsh CfW's explicit mental-health help-seeking framing appears at PS3 (Bands C–D), 2 bands later than REAL's introduction. Circle Solutions does not have a dedicated help-seeking strand until Year 12 ("I can identify differences between various emotional states and recognize when to seek support"), which is notably late relative to REAL, CASEL, and CfW.

**Community engagement and civic purpose.** REAL sequences community contribution from Band A (classroom job, LT 5.2) through documented project impact (Band D) to leading sustained initiatives (Band E) to articulating purpose and legacy (Band F). CASEL's parallel progression goes from collaborative idea-sharing (K-1) through community problem-solving (Grade 4-5) to active community contribution (Grade 6-8) to community well-being orientation with impact evaluation (Grade 11-12). The two frameworks are closely sequenced on this strand across all six bands. Circle Solutions addresses community through its social justice strand (Year 9 and Year 12, Dim 11) with social justice action projects. Welsh CfW addresses community through pro-social advocacy at PS5. RSHE has no explicit community engagement strand.

## 5. Questions for PLC

1. **Grooming and exploitation timing.** RSHE treats grooming awareness as a primary-phase safeguarding topic (End of Primary, Bands A–C), while REAL's explicit treatment of "coercion, grooming, or exploitation risk" appears at Band E (LT 4.1). Neither CASEL nor Circle Solutions address grooming across any of their bands. Should REAL introduce a lighter-touch, age-appropriate version of this content at Bands B or C? What would a Band B version of grooming-awareness look like in REAL's dispositional voice?

2. **Online image-sharing law.** UK RSHE dedicates multiple items at End of Secondary (Bands C–E) to the criminal law around indecent images, sextortion, and AI-generated imagery. None of CASEL, Circle Solutions, or Welsh CfW address these as distinct curricular topics. REAL does not address them either. Given that REAL students are of secondary age (Bands C–F), does the team believe this legal knowledge is adequately addressed through REAL's consent strand (LT 4.1) or through pastoral/tutor systems — or is there a gap in REAL's coverage?

3. **Meaning and flourishing.** Circle Solutions' Dimension 12 (Meaning and spirituality) runs from Year 2 through Year 12 with explicit assessable learning targets for what makes life go well — "comparing short-term and enduring fulfilment" (Year 2), identifying a positive role model (Year 6), discussing what constitutes a good life (Year 9), and a personal values statement (Year 12). REAL has no equivalent strand. Does the wellbeing team believe that flourishing — not just regulation, relationships, and community — should be an explicit curricular object? If so, should it be added to REAL's framework, or is it addressed implicitly through LT 5.2 (purpose) and LT 2.2 (values)?

4. **Repair and restoration sequencing.** Circle Solutions introduces intentional repair language at Year 2 (Band A/B) — distinguishing accidental from deliberate hurts, participating in restorative processes. REAL's explicit relationship repair skill appears at Band D. Is the approximately 3-band gap intentional (REAL prioritises naming and regulating feelings first) or is it an oversight? Should Bands A-C include earlier repair language, even if the full repair skill is at Band D?

5. **Self-advocacy as a named strand.** CASEL explicitly names and progressively develops self-advocacy from Grade 2-3 through Grade 9-10 (Band B through E). REAL addresses help-seeking (LT 3.2) and communicating needs (LT 5.1) but does not name self-advocacy as a curricular object. Given that REAL students include international students who may need to navigate unfamiliar institutional systems, should REAL add a self-advocacy thread to LT 3.2 or LT 5.1?
