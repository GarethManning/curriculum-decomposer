## 3. Unique Content

### 3a. Unique to REAL (not substantively addressed in any comparison framework)

**Preserved from v3** (unchanged):
- **Focused Attention as a full A–F learning progression** (LT 2.1). No external framework develops attention as a systematic A–F competency. v4 adds **LT 8.2 digital-attention layer** (Bands C–F): persuasive-design analysis applied to personal attention management. This strengthens REAL's distinctive position in T04.
- **Wellbeing Science & Neuroscience Literacy as an A–F curriculum** (LT 6.1). No external framework teaches neuroscience mechanisms systematically. v4 adds **LT 4.4 emotional/relational-development science** at Bands B–F: biological/psychological basis of attraction, rejection, loss; multi-framework relational analysis at F. This extends REAL's neuroscience curriculum into an emotional-science companion.
- **Health & Evidence Literacy as an A–F curriculum** (LT 6.2). Source credibility from Band A; systematic review interpretation at Band F. v4 adds **LT 8.1 digital-information-literacy**: A–F verification methods including SIFT (D), platform algorithmic awareness (E), AI/synthetic disinformation detection (F). Two-LT REAL curriculum on evidence literacy is the most systematic of the five frameworks.
- **Metacognition as an A–F curriculum** (LT 7.1 + LT 7.2).
- **Puberty, Sexual Health & Safe Choices from an evidence and rights perspective** (LT 4.2 Bands D–F).

**New to v4**:
- **Cultural self-awareness as interrogative (not affirmative) identity work** (LT 1.3 A–F). No external framework frames identity work as critical cultural self-examination — recognising the limitations of one's own cultural lens and engaging with uncomfortable challenges. CASEL and Circle Solutions cover identity as strengths, values, and aspirations (affirmative orientation). Welsh CfW covers identity-shaped-by-groups at PS4–PS5 only. LT 1.3 is distinctive in asking students not merely to describe themselves but to recognise and sit with the contested nature of the self.
- **Digital influence and psychological agency as a T2 curriculum** (LT 8.2 Bands C–F). No external framework teaches persuasive-design mechanism, algorithmic curation, and digital psychological-incentive analysis as a systematic progression. RSHE touches social-media influence at EoS; the LT 8.2 sequence (design-feature analysis at C → attention/emotion/behaviour framework at D → algorithmic platform evaluation at E → cumulative-effect critical analysis at F) is unique.
- **Information verification as a T2 curriculum** (LT 8.1 Bands A–F). No external framework teaches source verification as a systematic A–F practice. LT 8.1 F's framing (AI/synthetic disinformation; boundary of what cannot be known) is particularly distinctive.
- **Digital assertiveness and wellbeing practice as a T3 dispositional curriculum** (LT 8.3 Bands A–F). No external framework treats digital boundary-keeping and assertive online communication as a systematic dispositional progression. The sequencing from adult-directed safety (A) → independent boundary-keeping (C) → boundaries-under-pressure (D) → sustained-when-unwatched (E) → values-expression (F) is distinctive.
- **Bystander reasoning as a systematic A–F curriculum** (LT 4.3). RSHE covers the propositional content of bullying/discrimination; CASEL and CS address cultural injustice at higher bands; but no external framework teaches **bystander response reasoning** (the four bystander options, safety assessment, group/power dynamics, institutional-conditions analysis) as a systematic progression. LT 4.3 is distinctive in its focus on the student's own reasoning about whether and how to intervene.
- **Emotional Self-Management as a T3 observational disposition** (LT 4.5). While CASEL Self-Management and CS Dimension 3 closely parallel LT 4.5 behaviourally, LT 4.5's T3 observation-indicator approach (multi-context observation, co-regulation at A, initiated repair at D, pattern-articulation at F) provides assessment-ready behavioural anchors comparable only to CS's Year-by-Year framework.

### 3b. Unique to UK RSHE (preserved from v3)

- Family diversity and different family structures (statutory requirement; T16)
- Legal framing of consent, abuse, grooming, and discrimination
- Explicit contraception and STI factual knowledge (Bands D–E) — LT 4.2 addresses these from rights-based perspective
- Digital safety/online risks — **revised in v4**: RSHE still provides the systematic statutory coverage A–E, but REAL now covers this territory systematically too via LT 8.x. The distinctive RSHE content is the *legal* framing (online abuse reporting, digital-footprint obligations) rather than the *capability* framing.

### 3c. Unique to Welsh CfW Health & Well-being AoLE (preserved from v3)

- Physical activity and movement as a curriculum domain (all PS — T19)
- Nutrition and food literacy as a standalone progression (PS1–PS5 — T20)

### 3d. Unique to CASEL SEL Skills Continuum (preserved from v3, with revisions)

- Growth mindset and self-efficacy as a full A–F progression (†Understanding Growth Mindset through †Sustaining Growth-Oriented Self-Perception). Still not present in REAL.
- Academic goal-setting and achievement planning (†Goal Setting and Achievement Planning) — more granular than REAL LT 2.2 or LT 7.2.
- **Personal identity and self-concept** — **revised in v4**: CASEL still covers self-identity development A–F systematically, but REAL now covers adjacent territory via LT 1.3. The distinctive CASEL content is the **strengths-and-aspirations** framing that LT 1.3 does not adopt.

### 3e. Unique to Circle Solutions SEL Framework (preserved from v3)

- Restorative justice and making amends (Dimension 10: acknowledging hurt, 'doing sorry', responsibility pie-charts, truth-and-reconciliation at Y12). REAL LT 1.2 D addresses repair; LT 4.5 D adds initiated repair; but restorative-justice as a named framing remains distinctive to CS.
- Meaning, purpose, and existential wellbeing at younger bands (Dimension 12 Y9: "what it means to live a good life"; photo portfolio of meaningful things). REAL places purpose at F (LT 5.2).
- Social justice action planning (Dimension 11 Y12: produce an action plan for a specific equity issue). REAL LT 4.3 F asks for analysis of bystander conditions; CS Y12 asks for an action plan — complementary but distinct.

## 4. Sequencing Differences

**REAL sequences attention earlier and more explicitly than any other framework** — preserved from v3. LT 2.1 begins at Band A. v4 adds LT 8.2 digital-attention layer from Band C.

**REAL sequences neuroscience content 2–3 bands earlier than any external framework** — preserved from v3. Fight/flight/freeze at Band B; amygdala-prefrontal interaction at Band C. v4 adds LT 4.4 B–F emotional/relational-development science as a companion.

**REAL sequences health evidence literacy 3+ bands earlier than RSHE** — preserved from v3. Source credibility at Band A. v4 adds LT 8.1 A–F digital-literacy, maintaining the early-sequencing pattern.

**REAL enters social justice and structural advocacy later than Circle Solutions** — modified in v4. REAL's systemic advocacy content (LT 1.2 F; LT 5.2 E–F; LT 4.3 D–F) now begins at Band D via LT 4.3. Circle Solutions still enters rights-and-responsibilities at Year 2 (Bands A–B). Gap reduced from 4+ bands to 3 bands.

**RSHE and REAL now have comparable online-safety sequencing** — new in v4. RSHE covers online risk, respect, and social-media influence A–E. REAL covers digital-content verification, persuasive-design awareness, and assertive-practice A–F via LT 8.x. The frameworks now align sequentially; differences are in emphasis (RSHE focuses on statutory risk/reporting; REAL focuses on capability-building across epistemic, psychological, and practical dimensions).

**RSHE family diversity expectation at Band A; REAL still has no content** — preserved from v3. For schools under RSHE jurisdiction, this remains a complementarity.

**REAL and Circle Solutions now converge on Band F identity work with different orientations** — new in v4. LT 1.3 F asks for engagement-with-challenge; CS Y12 (Dimension 1) asks for articulation-of-aspiration. Both are Band F identity work; they teach different things.

**REAL bystander and discrimination sequencing now matches RSHE but with different framing** — new in v4. RSHE covers bullying/discrimination A–E with increasing legal specificity. LT 4.3 A–F focuses on bystander reasoning and conditions-analysis. RSHE Band D adds criminal framing (LT 4.3 does not); LT 4.3 F adds institutional-conditions analysis (RSHE does not).

## 5. Questions for PLC

1. **The identity gap is closed — but in which direction?** v3 flagged T15 as a REAL structural gap. v4 closes it with LT 1.3 (cultural self-awareness, interrogative framing). CASEL and Circle Solutions continue to treat identity as strengths/values/aspirations (affirmative framing). The PLC question is no longer "does REAL have identity content?" but: **is LT 1.3's interrogative cultural-lens framing the right identity work for REAL students to do?** Should an affirmative strengths-identity progression also be added, or is the interrogative framing deliberately positioned as the distinctive REAL orientation? *(v3 PLC Q1 retired; replaced by this.)*

2. **The growth mindset gap is preserved.** CASEL's explicit growth mindset progression A–F is still absent from REAL. LT 7.2 (Self-Direction in Practice) covers adjacent dispositional territory but does not name or teach growth mindset as a construct. Same PLC question as v3: deliberate (REAL's metacognitive framing subsumes growth mindset) or unexamined? *(preserved from v3 PLC Q2.)*

3. **The online safety gap is closed — but does REAL's distinctive framing match need?** v3 flagged T17 as a REAL gap. v4 closes it, and REAL now has the most systematic digital-wellbeing progression of the five frameworks. The PLC question is: **has REAL's three-track framing (LT 8.1 epistemic / LT 8.2 psychological / LT 8.3 practical) been validated against actual student need?** Is the sequencing (LT 8.1 at all bands; LT 8.2 from C; LT 8.3 at all bands) developmentally appropriate? The coherence across C8's three LTs was designed recently (REAL-8 sessions) and has not yet been field-tested. *(replaces v3 PLC Q3.)*

4. **Social justice sequencing is partially changed.** LT 4.3 enters REAL at Band A (bystander reporting); systematic discrimination analysis enters at Band D. CS begins rights-and-responsibilities at Year 2. PLC question: **with LT 4.3 now in place, is the remaining Band A–C gap (from CS's explicit rights framing) still meaningful?** LT 4.3's Bands A–C focus on practical response (tell a trusted adult; choose a helpful action; assess safety).
