# Scope and Sequence: REAL School Budapest Wellbeing Programme

**Programme:** REAL School Budapest Wellbeing Programme — 8 competencies, 19 LTs, spanning the full school journey from age 5 to age 17. C1 Emotional Intelligence (LT 1.1, 1.2, 1.3); C2 Attention & Reflective Decision-Making (LT 2.1, 2.2); C3 Physical Wellbeing & Self-Care (LT 3.1, 3.2); C4 Consent, Safety & Healthy Relationships (LT 4.1, 4.2, 4.3); C5 Community, Purpose & Belonging (LT 5.1, 5.2); C6 Wellbeing Science & Literacy (LT 6.1, 6.2); C7 Metacognitive Self-Direction (LT 7.1, 7.2); C8 Critical Digital Literacy (LT 8.1, 8.2, 8.3).

**Developmental bands:** A (ages 5–7, Water + Air Dragons); B (ages 7–9, Earth Dragons); C (ages 9–11, Fire Dragons); D (ages 11–13, Metal Dragons + Light Dragons G7–8 entry); E (ages 13–15, Light Dragons G9–10); F (ages 15–17, Post-Metal G11–12).

**Intended outcomes:** Students develop emotional intelligence, self-regulation, physical wellbeing, healthy relationships, interpersonal skills, wellbeing literacy, metacognition, and critical digital literacy across the full school journey — culminating at Band F in identity-level integrated dispositions (LT 1.1, 3.2, 5.2, 7.2, 8.3), sophisticated analytical capabilities (LT 1.3, 2.2, 4.1, 4.2, 4.3, 6.2, 7.1, 8.1, 8.2), and a foundational wellbeing-science knowledge base (LT 6.1).

**Time available:** 90 minutes per week confirmed at Grade 7 (Band D). Weekly block at other bands to be confirmed.

**Input state:** State A — prerequisite_map, kud_charts, and lt_types all supplied with programme-specific sequencing principles. Highest confidence.

## 1. Knowledge Architecture Diagnosis

### Hierarchical elements (T1 — prerequisite-driven)

- **LT 6.1 Brain, Body & Wellbeing Science** is the single T1 spine of the programme. Bands A → B → C → D → E → F form a cumulative vocabulary chain (body signals → fight/flight/freeze + habit loop → amygdala/PFC/neuroplasticity → HPA axis + system integration → allostatic load + adolescent-brain specifics → critical neuroscience literacy). Each band's content explicitly builds on the previous and downstream LTs depend on specific bands.
- **Embedded T1 Know layers within T2 LTs**: LT 3.1 A–B (body needs, habit accumulation); LT 4.2 B–F (puberty changes, reproductive biology, STI/pregnancy, health-system facts); LT 8.2 C–F (persuasive design vocabulary, algorithmic mechanics). These are local hierarchical chains within otherwise horizontal LTs.

### Horizontal elements (T2 — analytical reasoning, less strictly ordered)

- **LT 1.3** Personal Identity & Cultural Self-Awareness
- **LT 2.1** Focused Attention & Strategy
- **LT 2.2** Reflective Decision-Making
- **LT 3.1** Health Literacy & Habits (T2 with embedded T1)
- **LT 4.1** Bodies, Boundaries & Consent
- **LT 4.2** Puberty, Health & Safe Choices (T2 with embedded T1)
- **LT 4.3** Active Bystander & Anti-Discrimination (reasoning only)
- **LT 5.1** Interpersonal Skills & Communication
- **LT 6.2** Health Information Literacy
- **LT 7.1** Pattern Analysis & Adjustment
- **LT 8.1** Information Verification & Media Literacy
- **LT 8.2** Digital Influence & Psychological Agency (T2 with embedded T1)

These develop by progression levers (complexity, precision, reasoning, scope, transfer, independence) across bands rather than through content prerequisites. Ordering within a band is flexible.

### Dispositional elements (T3 — experiential readiness logic)

- **LT 1.1** Self-Awareness & Regulation
- **LT 1.2** Social Awareness & Empathy
- **LT 3.2** Self-Care & Resilience
- **LT 5.2** Community Engagement & Purpose
- **LT 7.2** Self-Direction in Practice *(metacognitive — light conceptual framing before practice may be warranted)*
- **LT 8.3** Digital Assertiveness & Wellbeing Strategies

LT 1.3 is classified as T2 but assessed dispositionally through observation indicators — it sits between the two categories in practice. These six (seven with LT 1.3) develop continuously across all bands where applicable and cannot be evidenced by single-occasion tasks.

### Architecture summary

Of the 19 LTs, **1 is purely hierarchical T1** (LT 6.1), **12 are horizontal T2** (three of which carry embedded T1 Know layers), and **6 are dispositional T3** (plus LT 1.3 as a T2-assessed-dispositionally hybrid). The programme is therefore architecturally **horizontal-dominant with a T1 spine and a substantial dispositional register**. This has three major implications for sequencing:

1. **LT 6.1 positioning is load-bearing.** Because it is the hard or soft precondition for 15 of the other 18 LTs, its placement at the start of each band and its reliable teaching are the single highest-leverage design decisions.
2. **T2 sequencing within a band is flexible.** Most LTs at most bands can run in any order as long as their prerequisites are in place. Planning can be driven by pedagogical pacing, thematic coherence, and specialist availability rather than strict LT order.
3. **T3 dispositions require cross-term pacing, not single units.** Six LTs (1.1, 1.2, 3.2, 5.2, 7.2, 8.3) must have ongoing development and observation opportunities throughout each band — not one unit per band. This is a fundamentally different scheduling shape from "one unit per LT."

## 5. Coherence Checks

### Vertical coherence

**Hierarchical (LT 6.1):** Every band's content logically builds on the previous band. A → body signals, sleep, food. B → adds stress-response physiology and habit loop. C → adds amygdala/PFC functional differentiation, neuroplasticity, mechanism-level habit loop. D → adds HPA axis, full neuroscience vocabulary, system integration. E → adds allostatic load, acute vs. chronic, adolescent-brain specifics. F → adds unresolved debates, critical neuroscience literacy. **No breaks.**

**Horizontal (T2 LTs):** Analytical sophistication genuinely increases across bands through named progression levers (complexity, precision, reasoning, scope, transfer, independence). Spot-check: LT 6.2 A (ask-an-adult) → B (source characteristics) → C (evidence types + bias) → D (evidence hierarchy + appraisal) → E (systems + funding bias) → F (systematic reviews + GRADE) — real cognitive progression, not "same thinking with harder content." LT 4.1: noticing + trusted adults → consent vocabulary → consent across contexts → power dynamics → legal + institutional → facilitation. **No breaks.**

**Dispositional (T3):** Development opportunities present throughout every band through ongoing observation design. One watch point — LT 5.2 is light at Bands A–B (classroom jobs, spontaneous inclusion) and concentrates heavily at Band D (Light Dragon capstone). Bands C and E carry less obvious scaffolding between these two anchor points. **Flag:** consider where Bands C and E community-engagement practice lives in a weekly rhythm, not only in project windows.

### Horizontal coherence

**Band A:** Heavy on T3 dispositional development (six LTs observed across daily classroom life) balanced by concrete T2/T1 content (LT 6.1 A, LT 3.1 A, LT 4.1 A, LT 1.3 A). Appropriate load. ## 6. Sequencing Principles

The sequence above applies the following principles:

1. **Hard prerequisite logic** — T1 hierarchical content must precede any LT whose content depends on it. Non-negotiable. (Bransford, Brown & Cocking, 2000.)
2. **Soft scaffold logic** — conceptual accelerators and soft enablers should be placed earlier where possible; when they cannot, the trade-off is flagged.
3. **Experiential readiness logic** — for T3 dispositional LTs, practice generally precedes the T1/T2 explanation of why the practice works. Students encountering the explanation as confirmation of lived experience rather than as abstraction retain and transfer better. (Kolb, 1984; Bransford et al., 2000.)
4. **Metacognitive T3 exception** — for LT 7.2 specifically, and to a lesser extent LT 1.1, light conceptual framing before practice may improve practice quality. Naming metacognitive strategies explicitly before use helps. (Flavell, 1979.)
5. **Counterargument to acknowledge** — explicit-instruction research (Kirschner, Sweller & Clark, 2006) argues novice learners benefit from explicit instruction before practice. The experiential-readiness default applies most strongly to social-emotional dispositional capabilities and should not be applied to T1 LTs. At REAL specifically, this means: LT 6.1 always teach first; LT 1.1 / LT 1.2 always practise first, explanation confirms.

**Programme-specific principles applied (from user):**

1. **LT 6.1 first in every band** — conceptual accelerator for the majority of LTs; hard prerequisite for LT 6.2, LT 4.2 C+, LT 8.2 C+, LT 3.1 C+.
2. **T2 precedes or runs alongside T3 pair** — LT 7.1 → LT 7.2, LT 8.2 → LT 8.3.
3. **LT 6.2 precedes LT 4.2 C+** — hard prerequisite.
4. **LT 1.1 and LT 5.1 hard prerequisites for LT 8.3 at B+**.
5. **Specialist delivery required** — LT 4.1 C–F, LT 4.2 B–F.
6. **Developmentally N/A cases respected** — LT 4.2 Band A; LT 8.2 Bands A–B.

**Review these principles. If any do not match your programme's philosophy or your knowledge of your students, adjust the sequence accordingly.** In particular, (a) principle 4 treats LT 5.1 B+ as hard prereq for LT 8.3 B+ — at Band B this depends on how much LT 5.1 B material is needed before digital assertive language can be transferred; (b) the metacognitive exception for LT 7.2 could be applied earlier or later depending on how much metacognitive vocabulary students are arriving with from their prior year.
