## KUD 14 of 19 — LT 6.2 — Health Information Literacy

**Routing note:** null

**LT Definition:** I can evaluate health information and health claims for trustworthiness, evidence quality, and bias — applying progressively more sophisticated analytical criteria across bands to make informed decisions about what to believe and act on.
**Knowledge Type:** T2 — Horizontal. Health information literacy applies analytical criteria (source credibility, evidence type, bias signals, evidence-base strength, systematic-review reading) to specific claims; reasoning quality — not a single correct answer — determines performance.
**Do Evidence Type:** Performance. Written analyses, comparative source tasks, structured evaluations, systematic-review interpretation tasks. Rubric-assessed on single-occasion tasks that can be repeated across the year with different claim material.
**Band Scope:** A–F. All bands developmentally appropriate.
**Prerequisites:** **LT 6.1 — hard prerequisite** from all bands. Evaluating a health claim about stress, sleep, nutrition, or mental health requires a working factual model of the relevant biological system; without it, "evaluation" becomes pattern-matching on authority cues.
