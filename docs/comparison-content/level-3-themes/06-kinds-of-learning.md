# Theme 6 — What kind of learning each framework names

How RSHE, Welsh CfW, CASEL, and REAL each describe the kind of learning the curriculum is for, with reference to LTs 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 4.5, 7.2, and 8.3.

---

The deepest architectural difference between these four frameworks is what they each take learning to be. RSHE specifies knowledge to retain and skills to apply by end-of-phase. Welsh CfW commits to four kinds of person to become, alongside competence-and-values bundles. CASEL frames competencies as abilities practised across nested settings. REAL specifies knowledge and skills like the others — and at six learning targets (LTs 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 4.5, 7.2, 8.3) adds a category recorded only when it shows up as a pattern across multiple contexts.

RSHE's foundational premise is that "children and young people need knowledge and skills that will enable them to make informed and ethical decisions about their wellbeing, health and relationships" [rshe-foreword §1]. The framework specifies content as terminal goals — what pupils should cover by end of primary and end of secondary. Effective teaching also cultivates "positive characteristics including resilience, self-worth, self-respect, honesty, integrity, courage, kindness, and trustworthiness" [rshe-foreword §1]. The framing is knowledge-and-skills-and-character. What the curriculum specifies is the content; sequencing and pedagogy are school decisions.

Welsh CfW commits to four overarching purposes — the kinds of person all learners should be supported to develop into: ambitious capable learners; enterprising creative contributors; ethical informed citizens; healthy confident individuals [cfw-four-purposes §4, §5, §6, §7]. Within Health and Well-being, RSE is framed as a bundle of knowledge, skills, the ways of being students develop, and values that empowers learners [cfw-rse-statutory-principles §5]. Progression runs as deepening competence — each phase consolidates and strengthens the same competencies, applied in new and relevant contexts [cfw-rse-statutory-principles §11]. The framing is becoming a kind of person, through revisited learning across phases.

CASEL describes social and emotional learning as "the process through which all young people and adults acquire and apply the knowledge, skills, and attitudes" needed to develop healthy identities, manage emotions, and make caring decisions [casel-framework-definition §1]. The five competencies are each defined as abilities — Self-Awareness as "the abilities to understand one's own emotions, thoughts, and values" [casel-framework-definition §5]. Competence is positioned as an ability practised across the four nested settings — Classrooms, Schools, Families & Caregivers, Communities [casel-framework-definition §10–§13]. The framing is knowledge-and-skills-and-attitudes acquired and applied through practice across settings.

REAL specifies knowledge and skills at every learning target like the others, but at six learning targets adds a category recorded only when it shows up as a pattern across multiple contexts. At LT 1.1 Band B, the pattern is "the student naming a recurring personal trigger — time of day, type of task, particular social configuration — and selecting a strategy from their practised repertoire before the trigger escalates, across more than one setting" [real-framework-overview §5]. At LT 7.2 Band F, the pattern is "a stable, articulated metacognitive identity — a consistent pattern of how they learn, reflect, and adjust — that they can describe to others without rehearsing it, and that persists across highly novel, high-stakes, or unfamiliar contexts without any scaffolding" [real-framework-overview §8]. The same recording standard runs through LT 1.2 (empathy after conflict), LT 1.3 (cultural self-awareness articulated without prompting), LT 4.5 (regulation in activated moments), and LT 8.3 (digital life as values made visible) — a band is established only when the pattern appears across multiple observed occasions, not on demand.

Each framework names a different kind of learning at its core. RSHE specifies knowledge, skills, and character outcomes. CfW commits to kinds of person to become, supported through deepening competence. CASEL frames abilities acquired and practised across nested settings. REAL specifies knowledge and skills at every learning target, and at six of them sets a different recording standard: a category that becomes part of the student only when it shows up as a pattern across contexts over time. Different answers shape what teachers do, what counts as evidence, and what each curriculum can promise.
