# Theme 3 — Identity and self-concept across contexts

How RSHE, Welsh CfW, CASEL, and REAL each treat the territory that REAL holds across LTs 1.3, 4.4, 5.2, and 8.3.

---

Identity and self-concept threads through every wellbeing framework, with different reach and shape. In REAL it appears across four learning targets: cultural-and-contextual self-awareness (LT 1.3), the patterns of emotional and relational development that shape who a person becomes (LT 4.4), the purpose-and-community identity that develops through sustained engagement (LT 5.2), and the digital identity made visible through what a student engages with online (LT 8.3). The external frameworks treat this territory with very different scope.

RSHE's curriculum is anchored in relationships, sexual health, and physical and mental wellbeing [rshe-foreword §1, §3]. Identity itself is not a curriculum thread. It appears once in the primary "Respectful, kind relationships" topic — pupils should "build a strong sense of their own identity, including through developing skills and interests" [rshe-pilot-extracts §36] — as a by-product of self-esteem rather than as a content area in its own right. Within-phase sequencing of identity-related work is left to schools.

Welsh CfW addresses identity through several routes. Purpose 4 commits learners to becoming "healthy, confident individuals" who "have secure values and are establishing their spiritual and ethical beliefs" [cfw-four-purposes §7]. WM4 names the social-shaping pathway: at PS3, learners recognise that "values, attitudes and identity are shaped by different groups and influences" [cfw-pilot-extracts §22]; at PS4 they "evaluate how my values, attitudes and identity are shaped by the groups and social influences with which I interact" [cfw-pilot-extracts §23]. Purpose 3's vision of "ethical, informed citizens who… are knowledgeable about their culture, community, society and the world, now and in the past" [cfw-four-purposes §6] adds a culture-and-community lens to identity formation.

CASEL frames identity within Self-Awareness — "the abilities to understand one's own emotions, thoughts, and values and how they influence behavior across contexts" [casel-framework-definition §5]. The grade-band continuum opens with affirmative content: at Grade K–1, students "list things they like (interests) and things they do well (strengths)" [casel-pilot-extracts §20]; by Grade 6–8, they "identify how interests, strengths, and values influence their academic and social opportunities" [casel-pilot-extracts §9]. The Transformative SEL extension situates identity within "creating equitable settings and systems and promoting justice-oriented school and civic engagement" [casel-equity-transformative-sel §1].

REAL's identity content is held across four learning targets. LT 1.3 reaches Band D where students name unchosen memberships — "the family they were born into, the nationality on their passport, the first language they grew up with… and these unchosen memberships shape how others respond to them and how they navigate the world" [real-framework-overview §28] — and at Band E identify, without prompting, how their cultural background might be limiting or distorting their interpretation of a text, event, or interaction [real-framework-overview §29]. LT 4.4 brings the relational-development science at Band F, including attachment theory examined for its strong empirical core, contested middle, and popularised periphery [real-framework-overview §16]. LT 5.2 reaches Band F where the teacher notices "the student's post-school choices (path, community involvement, roles) trace specifically to prior sustained engagement" [real-framework-overview §56]. LT 8.3 reaches Band F where digital life becomes "an expression of their values — what they engage with, what they create, what they refuse" [real-framework-overview §73].

Identity is the most philosophy-revealing of these threads. RSHE keeps it as a by-product of self-esteem. CfW frames it through Purpose 4's confident-individual vision and WM4's social-shaping arc. CASEL routes it through Self-Awareness as an ability to understand values across contexts, with Transformative SEL adding the equity dimension. REAL treats identity as something that becomes visible across four LTs at the upper bands — cultural-self-awareness, relational-development knowledge, sustained community engagement, digital-life-as-values. Each shape follows from a different premise about what a curriculum can ask of a person; the source documents trace the premises.
