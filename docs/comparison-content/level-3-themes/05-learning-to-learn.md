# Theme 5 — Learning to learn

How RSHE, Welsh CfW, CASEL, and REAL each treat the territory that REAL holds across LTs 2.1, 2.2, 6.2, 7.1, and 7.2.

---

Learning-to-learn is the broadest cross-cutting reach in this corpus, and the territory most directly tied to how a student develops as a learner over time. In REAL it spans five learning targets across three competencies: focused attention (LT 2.1), reflective decision-making (LT 2.2), health-information literacy (LT 6.2), pattern analysis (LT 7.1), and self-direction in practice (LT 7.2). Each external framework names parts of this territory but differs in what kind of object the reflection is about.

RSHE's foreword names character outcomes — pupils develop "positive characteristics including resilience, self-worth, self-respect, honesty, integrity, courage, kindness, and trustworthiness" through effective teaching [rshe-foreword §1] — without specifying metacognitive content as such. The framing is character-and-knowledge for end-of-phase. At secondary, decision-making appears in risk contexts — pupils learn "the physical and psychological risks associated with alcohol consumption" [rshe-pilot-extracts §28] and adjacent substance-use content. Attention as a cognitive resource, evidence-evaluation as a sustained skill, pattern-noticing about thinking itself, and self-direction without external prompting are not separately developed.

In Welsh CfW, learning-to-learn is most concentrated in WM3, the decision-making What Matters statement. PS3 has learners "make considered decisions, taking into account available information, including past experiences" [cfw-pilot-extracts §8]; PS4 builds evidence-evaluation — "research, examine and evaluate a range of evidence to make considered and informed decisions" [cfw-pilot-extracts §10]; PS5 reaches "critically evaluate factors and implications, including risks, when making decisions individually and collectively" [cfw-pilot-extracts §13]. Attention appears in WM2's mental-wellbeing arc — "I can focus my attention" at PS1 [cfw-pilot-extracts §1] — but is not developed into a strategy-and-conditions design skill.

CASEL distributes learning-to-learn across Self-Management, Self-Awareness, and Responsible Decision-Making. Self-Management at Grade 6–8 has students "reflect on progress toward a goal and evaluate the steps needed to achieve this goal" [casel-pilot-extracts §7]; at Grade 9–10, "monitor progress toward a specific goal by developing check-points or adjusting the plan as needed" [casel-pilot-extracts §13]. Responsible Decision-Making builds the procedural skill: "utilize the steps of a decision-making model" at Grade 6–8 [casel-pilot-extracts §10], and at Grade 9–10 "gather evidence and apply the steps of a decision-making process" [casel-pilot-extracts §16]. Self-Awareness at Grade 6–8 extends to "describe how emotions impact their thoughts, behaviors, and relationships" [casel-pilot-extracts §8]. Reflection in CASEL is mostly reflection on choices and goal-progress.

REAL builds an executive-function arc across five learning targets, with the reflective object differing across them. LT 2.1 reaches Band E where students "design the conditions for sustained attention in a piece of my own work — including managing digital distractions" [real-framework-overview §31]. LT 7.1 reaches Band F with a structured personal metacognitive practice — "an explicit, named set of strategies, prompts, and monitoring practices that a person has developed and tested across more than one domain" [real-framework-overview §19]. LT 7.2 reaches Band D where "the teacher notices the student articulating a specific adjustment they have made to their thinking or behaviour and describing its effect across more than one domain, without prompting" [real-framework-overview §66]. LT 2.2 builds reflective decision-making with a Band E focus on cognitive-bias awareness in the student's own reasoning [real-framework-overview §34], and LT 6.2's evidence-hierarchy work overlaps with the reasoning-quality strand at the upper bands [real-framework-overview §63].

Learning-to-learn is the place where each framework's choice of reflective object shows most. RSHE reflects on character outcomes — what kind of person the curriculum produces. CfW reflects on decisions and the social influences that shape them. CASEL reflects on goals, choices, and decision-procedures. REAL reflects on the thinking that produces the choices, the patterns that produce the thinking, and on whether a sustained personal practice has formed. Five learning targets carry that work in REAL; each external framework develops some of it under different headings, with different reach.
